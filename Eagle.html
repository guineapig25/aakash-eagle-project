Welcome to First Lego League!

FIRST LEGO League is an exciting global robotics program that introduces STEM concepts to children ages 9-16 (grades 4-8) through fun, hands-on learning. Teams compete in the Robot Game and present their solutions to real-world problems in the Innovation Project.

This guide is designed to help teams navigate through the key aspects of the FLL experience.

The Three Components of FLL

  1. Robot Game: Teams design, build, and program an autonomous LEGO robot to complete missions on a themed playing field.
  2. Innovation Project: Teams identify and solve a real-world problem related to the season's theme.
  3. Core Values: Teams demonstrate the FIRST Core Values throughout all they do.

Robot Design

The robot design component focuses on the mechanical design process, the physical robot, and its attachments. Teams are evaluated on their design process, mechanical efficiency, and innovation.

Robot Building Fundamentals

  • Base Robot: Design a sturdy, reliable robot base that can navigate accurately and consistently.
  • Attachments: Create modular, effective attachments to complete specific missions.
  • Balance: Consider weight distribution, center of gravity, and stability in your design.
  • Efficient Use of Parts: Use LEGO pieces thoughtfully to create simple, functional mechanisms.

Design Process

  1. Identify Problems: Analyze the mission requirements and identify the challenges.
  2. Brainstorm Solutions: Generate multiple design ideas before building.
  3. Build and Test: Create prototypes and test their effectiveness.
  4. Iterate and Improve: Use test results to refine your design.
  5. Document: Keep an engineering notebook to track your design process and decisions.

Design Tips

  • Use a robust drivetrain with good traction and control.
  • Consider using a consistent attachment mounting system for quick changes.
  • Test extensively on the competition mat under various conditions.
  • Focus on reliability over complexity â€” simple designs often work better!
  • Consider mission sequence and robot path when designing.

Programming

Programming brings your robot to life! Teams must create code that allows their robot to navigate autonomously and complete missions efficiently. Clear, well-organized code that demonstrates good programming practices is valued.

Programming Fundamentals

  • LEGO SPIKE/EV3/Robot Inventor: Learn the basics of your programming platform.
  • Algorithms: Develop step-by-step solutions to mission challenges.
  • Sensors: Utilize sensors for precise navigation and interaction.
  • Modularity: Create reusable functions or blocks for common actions.
  • Consistency: Ensure reliable performance through consistent code execution.

Programming Best Practices

  1. Start Simple: Begin with basic movements before adding complexity.
  2. Comment Your Code: Add notes explaining what each section does.
  3. Test Frequently: Debug problems as they arise rather than at the end.
  4. Use Variables: Create variables for values that might need adjustment.
  5. Organize Programs: Name programs clearly and organize them logically.

Advanced Techniques

  • PID Control: Implement proportional-integral-derivative control for precise line following or turning.
  • Gyro Navigation: Use the gyro sensor for accurate turns and straight drives.
  • Parallel Processing: Run multiple tasks simultaneously when possible.
  • Data Logging: Collect and analyze sensor data to improve performance.
  • Menu Systems: Create a mission selection menu for tournament efficiency.

Innovation Project

The Innovation Project challenges teams to identify and solve a real-world problem related to the season's theme. Teams research, develop an innovative solution, and share their ideas with others.

Project Process

  1. Identify a Problem: Research issues related to the season's theme and select a specific problem to address.
  2. Research Existing Solutions: Investigate how others have approached similar problems.
  3. Develop an Innovative Solution: Create a new or improved solution to the problem.
  4. Consult Experts: Seek feedback from professionals in relevant fields.
  5. Refine and Test: Improve your solution based on feedback and testing.
  6. Share with Others: Communicate your solution to potential beneficiaries.

Presentation Tips

  • Create a clear, engaging presentation that tells your project story.
  • Include visual aids such as posters, models, or digital presentations.
  • Involve all team members in the presentation.
  • Practice answering questions about your research and solution.
  • Show evidence of how you shared your solution with others who might benefit.

Project Documentation

Keep thorough records of your Innovation Project journey:

  • Research notes and sources
  • Expert interview summaries
  • Solution development iterations
  • Sharing activities and feedback received
  • Images, diagrams, or prototypes of your solution

Core Values

FIRST Core Values are the heart of the program. They emphasize teamwork, discovery, inclusion, innovation, impact, and fun. Teams are evaluated on how well they demonstrate these values throughout all aspects of FLL.

The FIRST Core Values

  • Discovery: We explore new skills and ideas.
  • Innovation: We use creativity and persistence to solve problems.
  • Impact: We apply what we learn to improve our world.
  • Inclusion: We respect each other and embrace our differences.
  • Teamwork: We are stronger when we work together.
  • Fun: We enjoy and celebrate what we do!

Demonstrating Core Values

  • Practice active listening during team discussions.
  • Ensure all team members have meaningful roles and responsibilities.
  • Celebrate each other's successes and support each other during challenges.
  • Help other teams when possible with a spirit of "coopertition."
  • Document how your team demonstrates Core Values throughout the season.

Team Building Activities

Regular team building exercises help strengthen Core Values:

  • Collaborative problem-solving challenges
  • Communication games that emphasize listening and clarity
  • Activities that highlight different strengths and skills
  • Reflection discussions about teamwork successes and challenges
  • Celebrations of individual and team accomplishments

Competition Day

Competition days are exciting opportunities to showcase your team's hard work and learn from others. Tournaments include robot game rounds, judging sessions, and team activities.

Preparing for Competition

  • Create a detailed packing list for all robot, project, and team materials.
  • Practice your Innovation Project presentation within the time limit (typically 5 minutes).
  • Prepare for the Robot Design explanation (how your robot works and why you designed it that way).
  • Review Core Values and how your team demonstrates them.
  • Test your robot on an official mat under various lighting conditions.
  • Create a pit display that showcases your team's journey.

Competition Day Schedule

While schedules vary, most tournaments include:

  1. Check-in and Pit Setup: Arrive early to register and set up your team area.
  2. Opening Ceremony: Official welcome and announcements.
  3. Judging Sessions: Scheduled times for Core Values, Innovation Project, and Robot Design presentations.
  4. Robot Game Rounds: Multiple opportunities (usually 3) to run your robot on the competition table.
  5. Team Activities: Fun collaborative activities with other teams.
  6. Closing Ceremony: Awards presentation and celebration.

Competition Tips

  • Create and follow a schedule to ensure you're on time for all sessions.
  • Designate roles for competition day (robot handlers, presenters, etc.).
  • Have backup copies of programs and presentations.
  • Bring extra batteries and charge devices the night before.
  • Practice quick robot repairs and troubleshooting.
  • Meet other teams and learn from their solutions.
  • Most importantly: Have fun and celebrate your accomplishments!
